Introduction of Rotary China’s Community Service Projects


Gift of Life / 生命的礼物


Historical Origins:


  • The Gift of Life project, which began in 1974, has grown into a worldwide project involving thousands of Rotarians and many other foundations, individuals, organisations and businesses. The project is dedicated to helping families that cannot afford life-saving operations for their child suffering from congenital heart disease.
  • The national data shows that the incidence of congenital heart disease is between 0.7% and 1.1% and in some high-risk areas, the incidence rate is between 1.37% and 2.5%.
  • More than 150,000 new-borns suffer from congenital heart disease each year.
  • About one-third of these children died in the previous month from complex malformations caused by congenital heart disease.
  • Another one-third of these children die as a result of worsening conditions or delayed surgery.

What has Rotary China done?

Over the past decade, Rotary clubs in Shanghai, Beijing and Chengdu have raised more than RMB20 million to fund and cure more than 1,000 of these children.

Children we have saved (identified by the local government as coming from poor families and identified by doctors as suffering from congenital heart disease)

  • 1/ Yan Qin, who came from a poor family and suffering from an open arterial catheter, was 10 years old at the time of the 2003 operation, has now grown up to be a healthy woman. (9 years after surgery)
  • 2/ Wang Kai, who was 18 years old at the time of surgery and suffering from Tetralogy of Fallot, was sponsored by the Rotary Club of York and Rotary Club of Scarborough, and is now married and has a healthy and lovely baby.
  • 3/ Our 100th child in the programme, Wang Leqiao (who was 13 years old at the time of the surgery in 2004 and suffering from ventricular septal defect), was sponsored by Victoria Beckham, is now in good health and has graduated from college last year.
  • 4/ Coming from a poor family, Du Honggang (who was 15 years old at the time of the surgery in 2005 and suffering from ventricular septal defect) received a donation of RMB1,000 from his primary school as travel expenses to Shanghai and is now in good health. (Photo taken 7 years after surgery)
  • 5/ Yao Zhenzhen, who travelled to New York for her treatment sponsored by the Rotary Club of Beijing, experienced the 9/11 disaster and survived. She is now a healthy and beautiful nurse. "Thanks to the Rotary Club of Beijing for giving me the life I have now and I will do everything I can to serve my patients," she said. She is currently married and has a healthy and lovely baby.

    • Sustainable Community Service Programme


    Screen: Free screening of congenital heart disease for children; providing surgical treatment as early as possible depending on the situation

    Train: Providing individual training for local organisations for women and children to raise awareness of congenital heart disease and the use of ultrasound instruments for diagnosis.

    Promote: Distribution of brochures and videos to raise awareness of congenital heart disease and promote new rural cooperative medical insurance.

    Visits by members of Rotary clubs for hospital exchange programme

    Distant training: Hubei congenital heart disease screening and science activities
